Their content engine already proves the thesis

They understand content-led authority at the institutional level. They simply teach no one, not their leaders and not their own faculty, to do the same for themselves. That is the single warmest hook Kaelon has.

04The finding

They publish what they do not teach.

Set their catalogue against their publishing, and the gap is plain: they are loudest on the very themes they teach least.

What they publish, and what they teach

Their loudest publishing themes and their largest teaching themes are almost two different lists.

They publish about the frontier, artificial intelligence, the future of work, well-being, creativity, and teach the establishment. The two lists overlap at a single point: sustainability.

Publishing order is a qualitative read of The Choice and the FT partnership; teaching is ordered by number of dedicated programmes.

THE GAP CLOSEST TO KAELON

Personal Brand and Digital Authority appear on neither list.

That is exactly why Kaelon leads with them. ESCP files them under careers admin and marketing, not leadership, so it neither publishes on them nor teaches them. They are the one discipline Kaelon is built on, the cleanest white space on the board.

Both empty shelves are the kind of gap ESCP fills by commission, through the Custom Solutions arm built for exactly this kind of external partnership. The opening is reachable.
05The proposal

Seven courses to build on top of their interests

Two moves, one rule: offer only where ESCP has a gap and Kaelon is strongest. Five ride the editorial momentum, the themes they publish about but do not teach; two fill the white space, the Personal Brand and Digital Authority shelf that sits on neither list. Des leads brand and authority, Sophie leads leadership and performance, both together where the course calls for it.

07Discipline

Two things not to pitch

The credibility of the whole pitch depends on only offering what Kaelon is genuinely the best at. These two are tempting and wrong.

✕
Leave it alone

Decision-making

They already address it, one core course plus a low-cost masterclass, and it is not where Kaelon differentiates. The only angle we could add, the human and identity side of deciding under uncertainty, is better folded into the Sustainable Performance course than pitched on its own.

✕
Not our lane

Sustainability & ESG

A heavy editorial theme and a tempting hook, but Kaelon cannot credibly deliver ESG content. Pitching something we would have to bluff would undercut everything else on the table.

09The route in

How to get in

Start from the relationship Sophie already has inside the school, lead with AI, then pitch the white space.

Step 1 · warm entry

Open through the relationship

Sophie already has a working relationship inside the school, so this starts warm, not cold. The natural first ask is low-friction: a module into Leadership for Diversity or Soft Skills for Agile Leadership, both Madrid-run, or the faculty edition of Digital Authority. No new programme required.

Step 2 · headline pitch

Lead with AI

The Leader's Edge in an AI Economy: their hottest live theme, a fresh angle, and the cleanest way to demonstrate what Kaelon does that no one else is offering them.

Step 3 · the flagship

Own the white space

Build the full Executive Personal Brand Programme the others ladder up to, then extend into the purpose-driven and performance courses.

Channel

The relationship opens the door; ESCP's Custom Solutions for Companies is how it gets formalised. They climbed into the FT top 10 for custom executive education in 2025, so the mechanism is open and they are actively investing in exactly this kind of external partnership.
